DeepPVMapper

Deep Learning for Rooftop Photovoltaic System Mapping

Gabriel Kasmi
Université Paris sciences et lettres
GitHub PhD Thesis Paper

Abstract

DeepPVMapper is a deep learning-based algorithm for mapping rooftop photovoltaic systems from overhead images. This work addresses the challenge of improving the observability of French rooftop photovoltaic installations through reliable deep learning models. The algorithm works in two stages: first, tiles from aerial imagery are classified to detect PV panels, then detected regions are segmented to extract precise polygons. From these polygons and auxiliary data, the system extracts key characteristics including tilt, orientation, installed power, and surface area of PV installations.

Overview of the DeepPVMapper approach for detecting and characterizing rooftop PV systems

Method

The algorithm processes high-resolution aerial imagery through a two-stage pipeline:

  1. Classification Stage: Aerial tiles are divided into patches and classified to detect PV panels
  2. Segmentation Stage: Detected regions are precisely segmented to extract polygon boundaries

The extracted polygons are converted to geolocated GeoJSON files, and system characteristics (tilt, orientation, installed power, surface area) are computed using auxiliary data sources. The system applies various filters to ensure quality, including restrictions to installations under 36 kWc located on rooftops.

Results

The DeepPVMapper system has been validated against the French national registry of installations (RNI), demonstrating reliable performance in mapping distributed rooftop photovoltaic systems. The algorithm successfully processes large-scale aerial imagery datasets and generates comprehensive inventories of PV installations with detailed characteristics.
